FAQs

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about our escrow service.

Escrow protects both buyers and sellers by holding funds securely until all terms of the agreement are met. The buyer pays into escrow, the seller delivers the goods or services, and only when the buyer confirms satisfaction are funds released to the seller. This prevents fraud and ensures both parties fulfill their obligations.

Our escrow service can be used for a wide variety of transactions including domain name sales, freelance work, vehicle purchases, digital assets, electronics, merchandise, business assets, and more. If you're conducting a transaction where trust is a concern, escrow can help.

Our fees are transparent and based on the transaction amount. Typically, fees range from 1% to 3% of the transaction value, with a minimum fee of $25. You'll see the exact fee before you commit to using our service. The buyer or seller can pay the fee, or you can split it between both parties.

The escrow process duration depends on your transaction terms. Typically, once payment is received and verified (1-2 business days), the seller can proceed with delivery. After delivery, the buyer has an inspection period (usually 1-5 days) to confirm satisfaction. Funds are then released within 1 business day. The entire process usually takes 3-10 business days.

If the buyer is unsatisfied with the delivery, they can file a dispute through our platform. Our dispute resolution team will review the case based on the agreed terms and evidence provided by both parties. We aim for fair resolutions and may mediate between parties or make a binding decision if necessary. Funds remain in escrow until the dispute is resolved.
